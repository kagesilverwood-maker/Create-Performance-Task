Upwind Sailing

Before we start learning about upwind sailing, let's do a quick review. Think back to the points of sail lesson, what point of sail is the closest to the wind you can get? A close haul! Now I want you to think back on the tacking lesson and remember that when we tack, the bow of the boat crosses the wind first and turns upwind.


Now that we've reviewed it, let's learn what upwind sailing actually is. When Upwind Sailing you have to be on a point of sail where the bow of the boat is more into the wind than not (a beam reach is the division).


The two most important components of upwind sailing are Angle and Changing Tacks. When going upwind, you can't sail straight up because that is the No-Go Zone, so you have to be at a close haul. Think of sailing upwind as driving on a highway. Your lane is the area right on the outside of the 45 degrees of the No-Go Zone and you want to stay in this lane until you change tacks.


When Changing Tacks you want to make sure to turn 90 degrees in order to be the most efficient. Your sail should stay at the same trim, just switch sides of the boat.

Racing Tactics


When racing, there are different tactics for different stages of the race.


The first stage is Pre-race. In this stage you first want to look at the wind conditions for the day and figure out if it will be consistent or shifty, gusty or sustained wind, and how strong and what direction it is coming from. This will help you be prepared when racing and allow you to find opportunities for an advantage over other boats. Then, sail out to the course at least 30 minutes before the start and sail the course twice- once on port tack and once on starboard tack- in order to find your land based references. Lastly, before the start of the race, you want to figure out what tack you want to start on based on the angle each one will give you (higher angle is better).


The next stage is the start and upwind leg of the race. Once you decide what tack to be on, time how long it takes to get from one side of the start line to the other. This will help you gauge where you need to be during the start sequence. Once the race starts, you want to be on the correct tack as soon as possible. While you are sailing upwind, make sure to stay relatively close to the middle of the course and avoid going on the far corners. To aid with tacking, look over your shoulder at 90 degrees. Make sure to keep the boat flat in order to reduce drag.


Once you go upwind, you will Round the Windward Mark. Before you even start rounding it, let the boom vang out. When you begin the round, make sure to dump the main in a controlled way and if you are on a slight reach, let the outhaul out (if you are at a dead run, keep it on). Depending on the wind speed, you should also raise the centerboard halfway.


When you get to the Leeward Mark the first thing you want to do is make sure the centerboard is down and the outhaul is adjusted for the new course. When you go to round, start out wide and then come in close. As you start heading up, pull in the mainsheet hand over hand to get it in fast. The sail should be all the way in and the jib trimmed accordingly.


As you come back upwind to Finish, make sure not to just follow the other boat's tacks. Once you start heading up, watch the other boats round the leeward mark to be able to cover them.
